    • vocabulary

    Click on the term to learn its definition.

    Ethnocentrism:

    evaluating another based on the standard of your own culture.

    Culturally relative:

    understanding that each culture is their own, and has different norms.

    Confirmation bias:

    the often unconscious act of referencing only those perspectives that fuel our pre-existing views, while at the same time ignoring or dismissing opinions— no matter how valid— that threaten our world view.

    Systemic racism:

    systems in place that create and maintain racial inequality in nearly every aspect of life for people of color

    Systematic racism:

    systematic racism is prejudice or discrimination methodically implemented according to a fixed plan or procedure against a race.

    Ageism:

    any criticism or negative sentiment that affects young or old people, regardless of validity.

    Cultural appropriation:

    the use of parts of a culture by someone who does not identify as a person from that culture. Although appropriation has been responsible for the spread of new and better ideas and technology throughout the world, it can be problematic when it is used as an "aesthetic". It is a controversial topic as those who adopt elements of an oppressed groups culture do so without giving credit. Additionally, while they may appreciate the culture, they remain prejudiced against its people.

    Model Minority Myth:

    the false perception that Asian Americans acheive a higher degree of socioeconomic success, therefore serving as an example to other minorities.

    Performative Activism:

    surface-level activism; activism that is done to bost one's social status rather than devotion to the cause

    Condesplaining:

    the act of a person said to be privileged explaining something to a person said to be oppressed in a manner believed to be condescending. In practice, there need not be anything inappropriate or condescending about said explanation.

    Intersectionality:

    the different types of privilege and oppression that a group may experience (i.e. being African American and being a woman).

    internalized racism:

    the acceptance, by marginalized racial populations, of the negative societal beliefs and stereotypes about themselves
