    • LGBTQ

    The month of June is known for being Pride month. Pride is celebrated to commemorate the Stonewall Riots which occurred during June of 1969. The month of June for many people of the LGBTQ+ community is a time to celebrate freedom and being yourself. Yes, there have been some changes since the Stonewall Riots but in some places LGBTQ+ rights are still being fought for. Currently, Poland is ranked one of the worst countries in Europe for LGBTQ+ rights. Rights for LGBTQ+ citizens in Poland have been limited. In America, the Trump administration is currently trying to place a rule where doctors are allowed to discriminate against LGBTQ+ patients. Last year the house had passed the Equality Act. This allows people of the LGBTQ+ community to be protected from discrimination in places of work, school, and home. Currently there is no information on when the Equality Act will go before the senate for a vote.
